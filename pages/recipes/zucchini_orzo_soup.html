Zucchini Orzo Soup

55 min.   |   178 kcal.   |   Soup

← RECIPES LEARN MORE HOME →

Introduction!

If you have been looking for an easy, delicious, and healthy soup recipe, look no further than this vegan/vegetarian orzo soup with zucchini and vegetables. It is hearty, flavorful, and filling. Plus, it is packed with nutrients and vitamins. The zucchini and carrots are cooked down to the perfect tenderness, and the orzo pasta adds a nice hearty element to the soup. The tomatoes add a touch of acidity and sweetness and the herbs give the soup a beautiful flavor. This soup is truly a powerhouse of flavor and nutrition!


WHY YOU WILL LOVE THIS RECIPE:

  • SATISFYING WITHOUT BEING HEAVY. This orzo soup is the perfect meal for those days when you want something hearty and filling, but do not want to feel weighed down. It is packed with vegetables, so you will get a good dose of fiber and nutrients and the orzo pasta provides some complex carbs to help fill you up. But overall, this soup is light and refreshing!

  • SIMPLE & EASY TO MAKE. This soup comes together quickly and easily and it can be easily tailored to whatever vegetables you have on hand. So if you need to use up some vegetables in your fridge, this is the perfect soup for that!

  • A TEXTURE WONDERLAND. This soup has a variety of textures that make it interesting and fun to eat. The orzo pasta is perfectly cooked, the zucchini and carrots are tender, and the tomatoes add a lovely burst of acidity!

    • Ingredients!

      ZUCCHINI ORZO SOUP

      PREP. TIME: 15 min. | COOK TIME: 40 min. | SERVINGS: 6 servings

    • 2 tbsp. Olive Oil
    • 1 Yellow Onion (medium, chopped)
    • 3 Celery Stalks (cut crosswise into ¼-inch slices)
    • 3 Carrots (halved lengthwise, and cut crosswise into ¼-inch slices)
    • 2 cloves Garlic (finely chopped)
    • 2 tbsp. Tomato Paste
    • 1 tsp. Oregano
    • 1 tsp. Salt
    • ½ tsp. Black Pepper
    • 6 cups Vegetable Broth (low sodium)
    • 1 x 14.5 oz. Canned Tomato (diced)
    • ½ cup Orzo (dried)
    • 2 Zucchinis (halved lengthwise, and cut crosswise into ¼-inch slices)
    • Fresh Basil Leaves (for serving)
  • FRESH VEGETABLES: Zucchini, carrots, celery, and onion round out this soup. Each vegetable brings its own unique flavor profile to the broth and to each bite. Feel free to add in any other vegetables you have on hand.


  • ORZO: You want to use a good quality orzo for this soup. Due to its shape, it is easy to get orzo confused with rice. That being said, orzo is a type of pasta made from semolina flour. It has a delicate, slightly sweet flavor that pairs well with the soup's vegetables and broth.


  • TOMATOES: A can of diced tomatoes adds acidity, sweetness, and a beautiful red color to the soup. Adding tomato paste to this soup helps bring forward the acid of the tomato.


  • VEGETABLE BROTH: To keep this soup vegan/vegetarian, you will want to use a good quality vegetable broth. It is recommended to use low sodium vegetable broth so that you can control the amount of salt in the soup.


  • SEASONING: Fresh garlic, dried oregano, salt and pepper really enhance the flavors of the soup.

    • Instructions!

  • STEP ONE: In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion, celery and carrot, and cook for 5-6 minutes until softened.


  • STEP TWO: Stir in the garlic, tomato paste, oregano and salt, and cook until the tomato paste is darkened and starting to stick to the bottom of the pot.


  • STEP THREE: Stir in the vegetable broth and diced tomatoes.


  • STEP FOUR: Bring to a boil, then add the orzo and simmer for 5-6 minutes until almost tender.


  • STEP FIVE: Add the zucchini and cook for 5 minutes until the zucchini is tender and the orzo is fully cooked.


  • STEP SIX: Remove from heat and allow to rest for 20 minutes until the orzo thickens. Taste and season with additional salt if necessary.




  • N.B. Cut the carrots and zucchini into uniform bite-size pieces to avoid some pieces being over or underdone.

    • FINAL STEP: Top with fresh basil and you're ready to serve!

    Cooking Tips!

  • KEEP IN MIND THAT THE SOUP WILL GET THICKER WHEN IT SETS . When it is done cooking, you may not notice the orzo in the soup. But the orzo pasta will continue to absorb the soup broth as it sits, becoming softer and thickening the soup.

  • MAKE THE SOUP AHEAD OF TIME. If you want to make this soup ahead of time, it is recommended stopping after you have added the vegetable stock and diced tomatoes. When you are ready to serve, heat up the soup in a pot, and cook the orzo and zucchini fresh. This will take less than 10 minutes, and the soup will be much tastier without any soggy pasta.

  • DO NOT BE AFRAID TO ADD MORE SALT. Depending on the brand of vegetable stock, this soup may need quite a bit of salt added at the end. So do not forget to taste before serving and season as necessary

  • CUT THE ZUCCHINI & CARROT INTO BITE-SIZED PIECES. This will help them cook evenly and ensure that each bite has a nice mix of vegetables.

    • Substitutions & Additions!

      SUBSTITUTIONS

    • SUBSTITUTE THE ORZO FOR RICE. If you are not a fan of pasta, you could easily substitute the orzo for rice. Just cook the rice according to the package instructions and add it to the soup at the end.

    • SWITCH UP THE FRESH HERBS. For this recipe, fresh basil was used, but other great options include oregano, or thyme.

      ADDITIONS

    • ADD PROTEIN TO THE MIX. This soup is delicious as is, but you could also add some protein to make it a complete meal. Some cooked chicken, shrimp, or tofu would be great additions.

    • ADD SOME SPICE. If you want a little bit of heat, add a pinch of crushed red pepper flakes or some diced jalapeño to the soup.

    • MAKE IT CREAMY. For a creamy version, stir in some heavy cream or half and a half after cooking. This will make the soup richer.

    • CITRUS IT UP. If you like lemon in your orzo soup, feel free to add the zest of a lemon or some freshly squeezed lemon juice before serving.

    • SERVE WITH CRUSTY BREAD. This orzo soup is best served with some crusty bread to soak up all the delicious broth.

    FAQ!

  • HOW TO STORE AND REHEAT ZUCCHINI ORZO SOUP?

    Leftover orzo soup can be stored in an airtight container in the fridge. When you are ready to eat, reheat the soup on the stove over medium heat until warmed through. You may need to add a splash of water or broth if the soup has thickened too much.


  • CAN I MAKE THIS SOUP WITHOUT ORZO?

    Definitely. If you want a grain-free version of the soup, simply omit the orzo and the soup will still be delicious.


  • HOW LONG WILL ZUCCHINI ORZO SOUP LAST IN THE FRIDGE?

    Orzo soup will last in the fridge for about three days. After that, the orzo will start to break down, and the soup will become mushy.


  • CAN I FREEZE ZUCCHINI ORZO SOUP?

    It is not recommended to freez this soup because the orzo does not hold up well when frozen. The pasta will become mushy and less enjoyable to eat. If you do choose to freeze it, make the base minus the zucchini and the orzo and just add them when you are ready to eat.


  • MY ORZO CAME OUT MUSHY. WHAT DID I DO WRONG?

    If your orzo came out mushy, it is likely because it was overcooked. Orzo only takes about 10 minutes to cook, so be sure not to add it to the soup until right before you are ready to eat. The cooking process will continue even when you remove it from the heat.
