Grilled Beef Kabobs

2 hrs.   |   423 kcal.   |   Main Dish

← RECIPES LEARN MORE HOME →

Introduction!

Grilled beef kabobs make a delicious and easy summer meal. This recipe calls for sirloin steak, bell peppers, and onions. The steak is marinated in a simple mixture of olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, cumin, paprika, cardamom, salt, and pepper before threading onto skewers. Serve with rice or grilled vegetables for a healthy and satisfying meal or for a Mediterranean twist, serve with a yogurt dipping sauce or classic tzatziki, and enjoy!


WHY YOU WILL LOVE THIS RECIPE:

  • TENDER. The marinade makes the steak cubes so tender that they practically fall off of the skewer!

  • FLAVORFUL. The combination of cardamom, cumin, paprika, and fresh garlic creates a delicious flavor that will bring you back for more!

  • EASY TO MAKE. This recipe is easy to follow and can be made in just a few simple steps!

  • CAN BE MADE AHEAD OF TIME. These kabobs can be made ahead of time and then grilled when you are ready to eat. This is great for summer BBQs or potlucks!

    • Ingredients!

      GRILLED BEEF KABOBS

      PREP. TIME: 2 hrs. | COOK TIME: 10 mins.  | SERVINGS: 4 servings

    • 1 lbs. Beef Sirloin (cut into 1-inch cubes)
    • ¼ cup Olive Oil
    • 2 tbsp. Lemon Juice
    • 2 cloves Garlic (finely minced)
    • 1 tsp. Cumin
    • 1 tsp. Paprika
    • ½ tsp. Cardamom
    • 1 tsp. Salt
    • ½ tsp. Black Pepper
    • 1 Yellow Onion (large, cut into 2 inch chunks)
    • 1 Red, Yellow or Orange Bell Pepper (cut into squares)
    • Fresh Parsley (chopped, for serving)

    Ingredients for Marinade

  • OLIVE OIL: The olive oil helps to tenderize the steak and also keeps it from sticking to the grill.

  • LEMON JUICE: The lemon juice adds brightness and acidity to the kabobs.

  • GARLIC: Fresh garlic is a must for this recipe since it adds depth of flavor to the kabobs.

  • CUMIN: Cumin is a classic spice that pairs well with beef.

  • PAPRIKA: Paprika adds a touch of heat and smokiness to the kabobs.

  • CARDAMOM: Cardamom is a warm and slightly sweet spice that gives these kabobs a unique flavor.

    • Ingredients for Steak Kabobs

  • SIRLOIN STEAK: Sirloin is the best choice when it comes to making steak kabobs. It is lean, flavorful, and affordable.

  • BELL PEPPERS: Using a mix of red, yellow, and orange bell peppers for these kabobs adds a pop of color and are slightly sweeter than green peppers.

  • ONIONS: Preferrably use sweet yellow onions because they have a milder flavor than white onions. However, you can use whatever type of onion you have on hand.

    • Instructions!

  • STEP SEVEN Allow the beef kabobs to rest covered in aluminum foil for 5 minutes before serving.
  • N.B. If you plan on using wooden skewers then soak them in a bowl of cold water for 30 minutes before using. If using metal skewers then no prep is required.
  • STEP ONE: In a large bowl, place the olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, cumin, paprika, cardamom, salt and pepper,


  • STEP TWO: Whisk well until thoroughly combined.


  • STEP THREE: Add the beef chunks and stir to coat with the marinade.


  • STEP FOUR: Allow the beef to marinate for at least 2 hours or up to 24 hours.


  • STEP FIVE: Meanwhile, preheat the grill on high heat then thread the marinated beef and vegetables onto the skewers.


  • STEP SIX: Place the beef kabobs on the preheated grill and brush any leftover marinade on top. Cook for 8 minutes total, flipping the skewers every 2 minutes.

    • FINAL STEP: Top over yellow rice or next to a salad, and you're ready to serve!

    Cooking Tips!

  • DO NOT SKIP THE MARINATING TIME. The spices really develop in the beef if you marinate it for a few hours or preferably overnight.

  • KEEP SPACE BETWEEN THE BEEF KABOBS ON THE SKEWER. Make sure you are not pushing them too close together, because this will ensure more even grilling. The vegetables in between the marinated beef will also help with this.

  • USE METAL SKEWERS IF POSSIBLE. Metal stainless steel skewers tend to adhere better to the meat, and there is less chance of wooden skewers chipping or the need to soak wooden skewers.

  • USE DOUBLE SKEWERS IF YOU ARE USING WOODEN SKEWERS. This is a helpful tip that makes it easier to turn the kebabs on the grill when cooking them.

    • Substitutions & Additions!

      SUBSTITUTIONS

    • USE FLANK OR ROUND STEAK. If you can not find sirloin steak, you can use flank or round steak for this recipe. Just be sure to slice the beef against the grain into chunks before marinating.

    • TRY OTHER VEGETABLES. If you are not a fan of bell peppers, you can try using mushrooms, zucchini, or summer squash instead.

      ADDITIONS

    • ADD SOME FRUIT. For a sweet and juicy addition, try adding chunks of pineapple, mango, or watermelon to your kabobs.

    • MAKE IT SPICY. If you like things spicy, try adding sliced jalapeno or red pepper flakes to the marinade.

    FAQ!

  • HOW TO STORE AND REHEAT GRILLED BEEF KABOBS?

    These kabobs are best served fresh off of the grill. However, if you have leftovers, they can be stored in an airtight container in the fridge. To reheat, simply grill or broil the kabobs for a few minutes until heated through.


  • WHAT IS THE BEST WAY TO THREAD THE KABOBS?

    Starting with a piece of beef, then alternate between vegetables is the best practice for a flavorful experience. This helps to keep the vegetables from sliding around on the skewer while also grilling the beef evenly.


  • HOW LONG WILL GRILLED BEEF KABOBS LAST IN THE FRIDGE?

    Properly stored, steak kabobs will last for up to three days in the fridge.


  • CAN I FREEZE GRILLED BEEF KABOBS?

    Yes and no. After the kebobs have been cooked, freezing them is not recommended as the vegetables will become mushy. However, you can freeze the raw beef cubes for later use.


  • WHAT IS THE BEST WAY TO CUT THE SIRLOIN BEEF?

    For steak kabobs, you will want to cut the beef into one-inch cubes. If your butcher does not have pre-cut sirloin, you can ask them to do it for you or do it at home. Just be sure to slice the beef against the grain into chunks before marinating.
