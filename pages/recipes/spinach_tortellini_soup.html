Spinach Tortellini Soup

35 min.   |   101 kcal.   |   Soup

← RECIPES LEARN MORE HOME →

Introduction!

This spinach and tortellini soup is a combination of tender baby spinach leaves, cheesy tortellini, fresh herbaceous basil, plump tomatoes, and a seasoning blend that pops. It is all simmered in a light vegetable broth for a soup that is hearty yet still light and perfect for a cool fall or winter day. Serve this with some crusty bread for dipping, and you have got yourself a meal!


WHY YOU WILL LOVE THIS RECIPE:

  • FLAVOR PACKED. This soup is loaded with flavor. The combination of fresh basil, onions, garlic, and crushed red pepper paired with tomato is simply irresistible!

  • QUICK & EASY. This soup comes together in just around 30 minutes, making it the perfect weeknight meal!

  • PERFECT FOR ANY OCCASION. Serve this soup as an appetizer or main course. It is also great for potlucks and parties!

  • CAN BE MADE AHEAD OF TIME. The base of this soup can be made ahead of time. In fact, the broth really comes to life the next day. When ready to eat, just reheat on the stove and add in the tortellini and spinach!

    • Ingredients!

      SPINACH TORTELLINI SOUP

      PREP. TIME: 10 min. | COOK TIME: 25 min. | SERVINGS: 4 servings

    • 2 tbsp. Olive Oil
    • 1 Yellow Onion (chopped)
    • 3 cloves Garlic (minced)
    • ½ tsp. Salt
    • ¼ tsp. Red Pepper (crushed)
    • 2 tbsp. Tomato Paste
    • 4 cups Vegetable Broth
    • 1 x 28 oz. Canned Tomato (diced)
    • ¼ cup Fresh Basil (chopped)
    • 1 x 9 oz. Packaged Cheesy Tortellini Pasta
    • 3 cups Fresh Spinach
    • ¼ cup Paremsan Cheese (grated)
  • TORTELLINI: You can use frozen or refrigerated cheesy tortellini for this recipe. If using frozen, there is no need to thaw before adding to the soup. Just add it in frozen.

  • SPINACH: Baby spinach leaves are preferred for this soup, but you can also use regular spinach leaves. Fresh spinach works best, but you can add frozen in a pinch.

  • BASIL: Fresh basil is a major part of the flavor profile of this soup. Dried basil just does not taste the same. That being said, you can use dried basil in a pinch, however, you may need to add more to get the flavor you are looking for.

  • TOMATOES: Canned diced tomatoes were used for this recipe, but you could also use canned crushed tomatoes. In addition, using tomato paste in this soup recipe really showcases the bright, acidic flavor of the tomatoes.

  • VEGETABLE BROTH: It is preferrable to use homemade vegetable broth, but you can also use the store-ready ones. If you go store-ready, get low sodium so you can have better control the salt in the soup.

  • SEASONING: In this soup, the fresh onion and garlic act as a flavoring agent in combination with the crushed red pepper and salt.

    • Instructions!

  • STEP ONE: In a large, heavy-bottomed pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion, garlic, and salt, and cook for 4-5 minutes until the onions are softened and translucent. Stir in the tomato paste and cook for 1-2 minutes until it darkens and starts sticking to the bottom of the pot.



  • STEP TWO: Add the vegetable broth, diced tomatoes, and basil. Bring the soup to a boil, then reduce the heat to medium low and simmer for 10 minutes.



  • STEP THREE: Add the tortellini and simmer for about 5 minutes until tender.



  • STEP FOUR: Stir in the spinach.

    • FINAL STEP: Sprinkle with parmesan cheese if desired and you're ready to serve!

    Cooking Tips!

  • MAKE IT AHEAD. You can make this soup ahead of time and refrigerate it for up to 3 days. Keep in mind, though, that once you add the tortellini and the spinach, it is best served immediately. If you want to prep in advance, cook the soup up until just before you add the tortellini. When you want to serve, reheat the soup base, then add the tortellini and spinach as stated in the recipe.

  • SEASON TO TASTE. Depending on what brand of vegetable stock you use, you might need to add more salt to the soup. Before you serve, taste your soup and add salt as necessary.

  • FREEZE THE SOUP BASE. This soup freezes well, minus the tortellini and spinach. It is a perfect freezer meal. To freeze, let the soup cool completely, then transfer it to an airtight container. It is recommended to use freezer bags. Label with the name and date, then place in the freezer for up to three months. When you want to eat, thaw in the refrigerator overnight, then reheat.

    • Substitutions & Additions!

      SUBSTITUTIONS

    • TRY A DIFFERENT PASTA TYPE. If you can not find tortellini or prefer to use another pasta, mini ravioli works great here. You dan also just use any short-cut plain pasta types like macaroni, penne, rigatoni, farfalle or orecchiette

    • SWITCH UP THE GREENS. You can use kale or Swiss chard in place of the spinach, or a combination of both!

      ADDITIONS

    • ADD MORE SPICE. If you like your soup with a little more kick, add an extra pinch or two of crushed red pepper flakes.

    • ADD PROTEIN. If you want to add some protein to this soup, cooked chicken or shrimp would be great additions.

    • ADD SOME CREAMINESS. If you want to add some creaminess to your soup, stir in half and half or heavy cream when you add the tortellini and spinach.

    • ADD PARMESAN CHEESE RIND. One best things you can add is parmesan cheese rind to the soup while it simmers. It adds so much flavor. Just make sure to remove it before serving.

    FAQ!

  • HOW TO STORE AND REHEAT SPINACH TORTELLINI SOUP?

    Leftover soup can be stored in an airtight container in the fridge. When you are ready to reheat, just place it back on the stove over low heat until warmed through. If you know you are going to have leftovers, only add as much tortellini as you think will be eaten and save the rest to add in upon reheat.


  • WHY IS MY TORTELLINI MUSHY AND OVERCOOKED?

    There are a few things that could cause this. One is that you allowed the soup to simmer for too long once the tortellini was added. The other possibility is that your tortellini was not fresh. I would highly recommend using fresh tortellini for this recipe if at all possible.


  • HOW LONG WILL SPINACH TORTELLINI SOUP LAST IN THE FRIDGE?

    Properly stored, this soup will last in the fridge for up to four days. After that, the tortellini will start to break down, and the soup will not be as enjoyable.


  • DO I HAVE TO USE CHEESY TORTELLINI?

    While this recipe was made specifically with cheesy tortellini, you could technically use beef stuffed tortellini if you prefer.


  • WHAT BREAD GOES BEST WITH TORTELLINI SOUP?

    Garlic bread works great with this soup, but any type of bread would also be good. A nice crispy slice of sourdough, or even homemade rosemary focaccia.
