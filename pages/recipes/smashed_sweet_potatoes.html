Smashed Sweet Potatoes

55 min.   |   222 kcal.   |   Side Dish

← RECIPES LEARN MORE HOME →

Introduction!

What is the hype with smashing potatoes? It is the thin edges that get all crispy in the oven because of the wider, thinner surface area. While you can not find baby sweet potatoes like you can with regular potatoes, you can easily slice sweet potatoes into smaller pieces before cooking and smashing them!


WHY YOU WILL LOVE THIS RECIPE:

  • CRISPY. It is difficult to make sweet potatoes crispy in the oven. Smashing them has been the best way found to achieve those crisp-edge results!

  • FLAVORFUL. The Italian seasoning, crushed red pepper, and fresh garlic bring a savory kick to balance out the natural sweetness of the sweet potatoes!

  • EASY TO PREPARE. This recipe is simple and does not require any extra or unusual ingredients. Plus, it is quick and easy to prepare and perfect for busy weeknights or last-minute dinner parties!

  • A DIFFERENT WAY TO EAT SWEET POTATOES. Smashing them adds a new texture and dimension to sweet potatoes, making for a unique and tasty side dish!

    • Ingredients!

      SMASHED SWEET POTATOES

      PREP. TIME: 10 min. | COOK TIME: 45 min. | SERVINGS: 6 servings

    • Olive Oil (as needed, for pans)
    • 4 Sweet Potatoes (long, scrubbed, cut into 1-inch-thick rounds)
    • 2 ¼ tsp. Salt (divided)
    • 3 tbsp. Melted Butter
    • 2 tsp. Italian Seasoning
    • 2 cloves Garlic (grated)
    • ½ tsp. Black Pepper
    • ¼ tsp. Red Pepper (crushed)
    • ¼ cup Parmesan Cheese (finely grated)
    • Fresh Parsley (for serving)
  • SWEET POTATOES: While you can use thick sweet potatoes for this recipe, they are harder to cut into slices and smash. It is recommended to use smaller, thinner sweet potatoes for easier preparation and cooking. Using organic sweet potatoes is also preferrable, but regular ones will work just fine.


  • BUTTER: The butter helps the seasonings stick to the sweet potatoes as well as adding a delicious richness to them.


  • SEASONING: Italian seasoning, crushed red pepper, and minced garlic add savory and spicy flavors to the potatoes. Salt and pepper also help bring out the flavors.


  • PARMESAN CHEESE: Grated parmesan cheese adds another level of flavor and a nice crispy topping. Optional, but recommended.

    • Instructions!

  • STEP FOUR: Sprinkle cheese on top of the potatoes and bake for 30 minutes, rotating the pans from top to bottom and front to back halfway through cooking time.

  • STEP FIVE: Remove from oven and let cool slightly before serving.


  • N.B. Thinner sweet potatoes are easier to handle and cut for this recipe.
  • STEP ONE: Preheat oven to 450˚F. then brush 2 large rimmed baking sheets with a thin layer of olive oil and set aside. Add sweet potatoes to a large pot, then cover with cold water by 1 inch. Add 2 teaspoons of salt and bring to a boil. Once the potatoes reach a boil, cook for 5 minutes, or until tender, test tenderness with fork. Drain and place on the prepared baking sheet.

  • STEP TWO: In a small bowl, combine butter, remaining salt, Italian seasoning, garlic, black pepper and crushed red pepper then pour the butter mixture over the potatoes and turn to evenly coat.

    • FINAL STEP: Sprinkle parsley on top, and you're ready to serve!

    Cooking Tips!

  • SCRUB, THEN SCRUB AGAIN. It goes without saying, but since all potatoes grow in the ground, they can be quite dirty. Still, it is hard to see the dirt on sweet potato skin, so you should usually let them sit in cold water for a few minutes to loosen the dirt, then scrub and rinse each potato twice before slicing them.

  • CHOP THE POTATOES INSTEAD OF SLICING THEM. If it is easier to chop the potatoes into 2-inch chunks, feel free to do that instead. You can still smash the chunks of potatoes for crispy delicious edges.

  • USE A SHARP KNIFE. Sweet potatoes are often harder to slice than regular potatoes, so make sure you have a sharp knife, but be careful while slicing.

  • BE CAREFUL NOT TO OVERCOOK THE POTATOES. When boiling the potatoes, you want to keep a close eye. You just want to be able to press them with the back of a cup. You do not want to cook them so long that the skin comes off; the skin makes them nice and crispy.

    • Substitutions & Additions!

      SUBSTITUTIONS

    • MAKE THEM SWEET. If you prefer a sweet version of smashed sweet potatoes, leave out the savory spices and top with a mixture of brown sugar, cinnamon and ginger instead.

    • MAKE THEM VEGAN. Simply leave out the parmesan cheese and replace the butter with a plant-based alternative like coconut oil or vegan butter.

      ADDITIONS

    • SPICE THINGS UP. The savory combination of Italian seasoning and red pepper flakes is great, but you can also get creative with the spice mixture.

    FAQ!

  • HOW TO STORE AND REHEAT SMASHED SWEET POTATOES?

    Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge. Reheat smashed sweet potatoes in a 375˚F. preheated oven for 10-15 minutes until warmed through. You can also heat them up in a skillet on the stovetop with a little bit of butter or oil over medium heat until warmed through.


  • CAN I PEEL THE SKIN BEFORE BOILING THE SWEET POTATOES?

    The skin is a big part of this dish as it adds a deliciously crispy texture. It is recommended you leave the skin on and scrub it well before boiling to ensure it is clean. That being said, you can peel them beforehand but you will not get that crispy texture.


  • HOW LONG WILL SMASHED SWEET POTATOES LAST IN THE FRIDGE?

    Leftovers will last in the fridge for about 4-5 days. They will not be as crispy after being reheated, but they will still taste delicious. Enjoy as leftovers or make into a yummy sweet potato hash. Add some diced onions and breakfast meats like turkey sausage to the skillet before reheating.


  • CAN I FREEZE SMASHED SWEET POTATOES?

    freezing smashed sweet potatoes is not recommended as they will lose their texture when thawing. However, if you do choose to freeze them, make sure they are fully cooled beforehand to limit moisture content and store them in a freezer-safe container.
