Baked Macaroni & Cheese

45 min.   |   598 kcal.   |   Side Dish

← RECIPES LEARN MORE HOME →

Introduction!

This easy baked macaroni and cheese is topped with panko bread crumbs. It is also made with gruyere and cheddar cheese with a little tang from dijon mustard and then baked, creating a creamy sensation from the inside with a crunchy crust on the top. In addition, it is quick and easy enough to serve as a weeknight meal. This is one cheesy side dish and a real crowd-pleaser. Kid-friendly and made from scratch!


WHY YOU WILL LOVE THIS RECIPE:

  • THERE IS NOTHING QUITE LIKE HOMEMADE MACARONI & CHEESE. Sure, you could just use a box mix and top it with bread crumbs, but this version is truly worth the little bit of extra effort. The combination of gruyere, cheddar, and dijon mustard adds depth to the cheese sauce and makes it so creamy and delicious!

  • BREAD CRUMB TOPPING. It adds a crunchy texture to each bite of macaroni and cheese, taking it to the next level!

  • QUICK & EASY WEEKNIGHT MEAL. This recipe is ready in just about 45 minutes and can easily be a main dish served with a salad or veggie side dish, or serve as a delicious side to your favorite protein!

  • KID-FRIENDLY, BUT STILL LOVED BY ADULTS. Macaroni and cheese is a staple for kids (and let us be honest, adults too!), and adding in the bread crumb topping takes it up a notch without compromising on taste for those picky eaters. Plus, it is made from scratch, so you know exactly what goes into it!

    • Ingredients!

      BAKED MACARONI & CHEESE

      PREP. TIME: 10 min. | COOK TIME: 35 min. | SERVINGS: 4 servings

    • 8 oz. Elbow Macaroni
    • 2 tbsp. Unsalted Butter (plus more to butter pan)
    • 2 tbsp. All-Purpose Flour
    • 2 cups Milk
    • 1 tsp. Dijon Mustard
    • Salt & Pepper (to taste)
    • 1 ½ cups Shredded Cheddar Cheese
    • ¼ cup Bread Crumbs (preferably Panko)
    • Parsley (for serving)
  • ELBOW MACARONI: Elbow macaroni is the quintessential pasta shape used in macaroni and cheese. However, feel free to use a different shape, just make sure to adjust the cooking time according to the box instructions.

  • BREAD CRUMBS: The bread crumb topping adds texture to each bite and an irresistible crunch. If you do not have panko on hand, try homemade bread crumbs or crushed-up butter crackers.

  • GRUYERE & CHEDDAR CHEESE: Both of these cheeses have a strong, sharp flavor that adds depth to the creamy cheese sauce. Gruyere adds a nuttiness and depth of flavor, while the cheddar adds that classic macaroni and cheese taste we all know and love. Grate them yourself for the best results.

  • DIJON MUSTARD: A little bit of dijon adds the perfect tang and depth to the cheese sauce. Do not omit this ingredient. It really takes the flavor up a notch.

  • ROUX: The roux is the base of your creamy cheese sauce. It is made with flour and butter, which helps thicken the sauce and adds a richness from the butter. Milk is incorporated to create a creamy sauce. Whole milk adds the most creaminess, but feel free to use a lower fat milk if preferred.

    • Instructions!

  • N.B. If your saucepan is oven-safe or you are using a cast iron pan, proceed normally. Otherwise, transfer it to an oven-safe baking dish.
  • STEP ONE: Start by making a roux. In a large saucepan over medium-high heat, melt the butter then add the flour. Whisk together for 2 minutes until they are well combined and golden.

  • STEP TWO: Slowly pour the cold milk, whisking as you pour to break up any flour clumping. Add the Dijon mustard and season with salt and pepper to taste. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce to low and simmer for 5-7 minutes, occasionally whisking until sauce is thickened.

  • STEP THREE: Add in the cheeses and stir them gently until they melt and become one with the sauce

  • STEP FOUR: Once combined, preheat the oven to 350°F. and Butter an 8 x 11-inch baking dish and set aside. Moreover, in a large pot of boiling salted water, cook the pasta al dente according to package instructions. Drain, rinse and set aside to cool.

  • STEP FIVE: After cooling, fold the pasta in the sauce, tenderly mixing so you do not break any pasta pieces.

  • STEP SIX: Once the pasta becomes fully combined in the sauce, transfer to the prepared baking dish and sprinkle with panko breadcrumbs. Bake for 20-25 minutes until the breadcrumbs are golden and the edges are bubbly,

    • FINAL STEP: Sprinkle parsley on top, and you're ready to serve!

    Cooking Tips!

  • COOK PASTA AL DENTE FOR TWO MINUTES SHY OF THE RECOMMENDED COOKING TIME. You want the pasta to be slightly undercooked because it is going to continue to bake in the sauce and in the oven. If you overcook it on the stovetop beforehand, it will likely get too mushy and break apart while baking.

  • COOL THE PASTA BEFORE TOSSING IT IN THE SAUCE. Normally we want to coat the warm pasta in whatever sauce we are making immediately. But doing so for baked macaroni and cheese can cause the elbow macaroni to absorb too much of the sauce and make it soggy after cooking. It is a good idea to rinse the pasta after cooking, drain and allow it to cool while you are making the sauce.

  • GRATE YOUR OWN CHEESE FOR THE CHEESE SAUCE. Store-bought shredded cheese is convenient and works well for topping the macaroni and cheese. However, it does not work well for the cheese sauce because it has anti-caking agents, which can prevent the cheese from melting efficiently and sticking together in the sauce. Also, it can sometimes have a powder residue or grainy texture from those caking agents.

  • USE PANKO BREAD CRUMBS FOR TOPPING IF AVAILABLE. Using regular bread crumbs works just fine too. However, panko bread crumbs give that extra crunchy taste.

    • Substitutions & Additions!

      SUBSTITUTIONS

    • USE DIFFERENT CHEESES. Gruyere is a great melting cheese and adds a delicious nutty flavor, but feel free to mix in some other cheeses like sharp white cheddar, gouda, fontina, or even some blue cheese.

    • TRY DIFFERENT TYPES OF PASTA. Elbow macaroni is classic, but you can also use shells, cavatappi, or even spiralized veggie noodles for a healthier option. Just make sure to adjust the cooking time accordingly for the type of pasta you use.

      ADDITIONS

    • ADD IN SOME EXTRA PROTEIN OR VEGETABLES. Cooked ground beef, turkey, or even chicken goes great with macaroni and cheese. Or add in some sautéed broccoli, mushrooms, or peppers for a bit of added nutrition. Just make sure to cook them beforehand before adding them to the macaroni and cheese.

    • SEASON YOUR BREAD CRUMBS. You can add some parmesan cheese, garlic powder, onion powder, dried herbs, or crushed red pepper to add extra flavor to the bread crumb topping.

    FAQ!

  • HOW TO STORE AND REHEAT BAKED MACARONI & CHEESE?

    Leftovers can be stored in the fridge, in an airtight container, or covered with foil. To reheat, place macaroni and cheese in a baking dish and cover with aluminum foil. Heat at 350°F. for 20 minutes or until warmed through. You can also microwave individual portions for 1-2 minutes, until warm.


  • WHAT IS THE BEST TYPE OF CHEESE FOR THIS BAKED MACARONI & CHEESE?

    This comes down to your preference. But generally, most recipes will have 2-3 types of cheese. Sharp cheddar and Gruyere is a very popular combination. You can make this with white cheddar, mild cheddar, or medium cheddar. If you can not find Gruyere, Jarlsberg Swiss cheese or regular swiss cheese works well. If you want to throw in a third cheese into the mix, it is highly recommended you use Raclette cheese, which is super melty and practically made for macaroni and cheese.


  • HOW LONG WILL BAKED MACARONI & CHEESE LAST IN THE FRIDGE?

    Leftovers can last in the fridge for up to 4 days. It is best to eat it within 2-3 days for the freshest and tastiest results.


  • CAN I FREEZE BAKED MACARONI & CHEESE?

    Freezing this macaroni and cheese recipe is not recommended. Since we are making everything from scratch, it is possible that the milk with separate or the pasta can become mushy when thawed and reheated. It is best to just make and enjoy within a few days.
